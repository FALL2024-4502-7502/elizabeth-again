22 books for late-discovered neurodivergents

By Elizabeth Derner

The neurodiversity movement has skyrocketed insight into neurodivergence, especially autism and ADHD. This has led many adults who were overlooked or misdiagnosed to grow crucial understandings about how their brains work.

But finding books about autism and ADHD involves navigating a sea of guides for neurotypical parents or therapists. These guides often focus only on children and promote pathology-based language and stereotypes, even if they mean to be supportive.

Many neurodivergent authors have been working to correct misconceptions. More and more books like the following aim to benefit neurodivergent adults and others.

Neurodivergence (general)

How Not to Fit In

By Jess Joy and Charlotte Mia

"How Not to Fit In" guides neurodivergent readers to better understand and embrace their authentic selves.

We're All Neurodiverse

By Sonny Jane Wise

"We're All Neurodiverse" challenges the "disorder" lens of neurdivergence and explains how society can be more accepting and inclusive.

Different, Not Less

By Chloe Hayden

"Different, Not Less" mixes AuDHD actor Chloe Hayden's personal experiences with support and advice for neurodivergent readers.

When the World Hurts Too Much

By Ash Banks

"When the World Hurts Too Much" offers strategies to help with mental health, including the intense overwhelm many neurodivergent people experience.

Divergent Mind

By Jenara Nerenberg

"Divergent Mind" explores how being neurodivergent often brings a complex mix of challenges and unique perspectives and strengths.

Neurodiversity and the Myth of Normal

By Kyler Shumway and Daniel Wendler

"Neurodiversity and the Myth of Normal" advocates for accepting all neurotypes and creating more inclusive environments and social norms.

Autism

Back to top

Unmasking Autism

By Devon Price

"Unmasking Autism" provides important insight into autism, unpacks the impact of masking and includes reflection prompts to help readers understand their own experiences more deeply.

Life as a Late-Identified Autistic

By Jackie Schuld

"Life as a Late-Identified Autistic" tells a personal story of discovering autistic identity in adulthood.

Self-Care for Autistic People

By Megan Anna Neff

"Self-Care for Autistic People" addresses the need for self-care strategies for autistic people specifically and provides several categories of tips.

The Autistic Survival Guide to Therapy

By Steph Jones

"The Autistic Survival Guide to Therapy" explains the barriers many autistic people face in therapy and ways to advocate for your needs in therapy.

What I Mean When I Say I'm Autistic

By Annie Kotowicz

"What I Mean When I Say I'm Autistic" features personal anecdotes that provide insight into the author's experience of being autistic.

Women and Girls on the Autism Spectrum, Second Edition

By Sarah and Jess Hendrickx

"Women and Girls on the Autism Spectrum, Second Edition" discusses autistic traits and experiences often overlooked or misunderstood due to boys' experiences forming common perceptions of autism.

ADHD

Back to top

ADHD is Awesome

By Penn and Kim Holderness

"ADHD is Awesome" addresses common ADHD-related challenges and encourages readers to acknowledge and celebrate their ADHD strengths and creativity.

The Year I Met My Brain

By Matilda Boseley

"The Year I Met My Brain" is an open and often relatable story of coming to discover and value ADHD as an adult.

How to ADHD

By Jessica McCabe

"How to ADHD" by YouTuber Jessica McCabe aligns with her videos in providing practical tips, important information about ADHD, supportive messages, and humor.

Your Brain's Not Broken

By Tamara Rosier

"Your Brain's Not Broken" is all about reframing negative perceptions of ADHD into positive, helpful ones.

Dirty Laundry

By Richard Pink and Roxanne Emery

"Dirty Laundry" shares honest discussions that address everyday ADHD-related challenges and intersecting mental health stigma.

Self-Care for People with ADHD

By Sasha Hamdani

"Self-Care for People with ADHD" offers strategies that aim to work with ADHD traits to improve well-being.

Other

Back to top

Laziness Does Not Exist

By Devon Price

"Laziness Does Not Exist" (by the author of "Unmasking Autism") advocates for understanding underlying factors that aren't "laziness" and not defining success by productivity.

How to Keep House While Drowning

By KC Davis

"How to Keep House While Drowning" provides tailored advice for managing and simplifying household tasks while struggling with mental health.

The Somatic Therapy

By Colton Mercer

"The Somatic Therapy" explains ways to understand and integrate somatic practices, which some neurodivergent adults find to be a particularly helpful type of therapy.

Heal Your Nervous System

By Linnea Passaler

"Heal Your Nervous System" covers techniques for calming the nervous system, which tends to be easily disregulated in neurodivergent people with sensitive nervous systems.
