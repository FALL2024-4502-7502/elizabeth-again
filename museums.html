7 unique music museums in the U.S.

By Elizabeth Derner

You may know about the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and Graceland, but the U.S. is home to a long list of music museums not everyone has heard of. Here are a few smaller or lesser-known places music lovers might enjoy.

filler photo

The Kazoo Museum

In Beaufort, South Carolina, you can learn all about the kazoo and even make your own at the Kazoo Museum and factory.

12 John Galt Rd, Beaufort, SC 29906

843-982-6387

filler photo

Dr. Ted's Musical Marvels

At Dr. Ted's Musical Marvels in Dale, Indiana, you can hear the music of antique mechanical instruments from around the world.

11896 S, 11896 US-231, Dale, IN 47523

812-937-4250

filler photo

Bayernhof Music Museum

You can also visit a mansion in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania to see historic automated instruments at the Bayernhof Music Museum.

225 St Charles Pl, Pittsburgh, PA 15215

412-782-4231

filler photo

V & E Simonetti Historic Tuba Collection

If you'd like to see more than 300 tubas, check out the V & E Simonetti Historic Tuba Collection in Durham, North Carolina.

1825 Chapel Hill Rd, Durham, NC 27707

919-599-3791

filler photo

Punk Rock Museum

For something more recent, visit the Punk Rock Museum in Las Vegas, which opened in 2023.

1422 Western Ave, Las Vegas, NV 89102

702-823-2983

filler photo

Polka Hall of Fame

Another genre-based museum is the Polka Hall of Fame in Euclid, Ohio.

605 E 222nd St, Cleveland, OH 44123

216-261-3263

filler photo

O Street Museum

While not exclusively a music museum, the O Street Museum in Washington, D.C. has music-related exhibits, including a famous signed guitar collection.

2020 O St NW, Washington, DC 20036

202-496-2020
